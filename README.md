**Project Description:**
Cardiovascular diseases (CVDs) represent a critical public health issue, accounting for the leading cause of mortality
globally and exerting a disproportionate burden on the United States healthcare system. According to the Centers for
Disease Control and Prevention (CDC), heart disease was responsible for one in every five deaths in the U.S. in 2022,
highlighting the need for early detection and risk stratification strategies that can inform preventive interventions [1].
Despite the availability of established risk models, the limitations of these frameworks in accommodating non-linear,
high-dimensional data have necessitated the exploration of more robust and adaptive methodologies.
This project employs Apache Spark, a powerful distributed computing framework, in conjunction with machine learning
(ML) algorithms to predict heart attack risk using a real-world dataset titled "Heart Attack Prediction in the United
States" from Kaggle [2]. The public dataset incorporates structured data on demographic, physiological, and behavioral
risk factors, providing an opportunity to model complex interactions that traditional statistical models often overlook.
The rationale for utilizing Spark lies in its capacity to handle large-scale data efficiently, enabling distributed data
processing, real-time computation, and integration with MLlib for scalable machine learning. This combination provides
a viable approach for transforming static risk scores into dynamic, personalized models that can capture nuanced patterns
across diverse populations. This project aims to explore the effectiveness of scalable machine learning models,
implemented using Apache Spark, in predicting heart attack risk from a large, real-world U.S. health dataset. By
leveraging big data technologies and advanced classification algorithms, the goal is to assess whether such approaches
can support accurate and data-driven cardiovascular risk assessment.
